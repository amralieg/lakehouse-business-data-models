[REVIEWER]  [2:02 PM]
Hi [REVIEWER], I reviewed the Travel & Hospitality model. Overall it looks great. Domain coverage, KPI depth, and the ECM/MVM split all make sense. A few things I wanted to flag:

 1. PII Tagging: Already in release notes as a known issue. Just making sure it stays tracked.

  2. RevPAR formula in ECM guest metrics (guest_stay_history): The RevPAR measure divides by occupied room nights, which is ADR, not
 RevPAR. A hotel person will catch that in a demo. I'd recommend removing it or renaming it since the correct RevPAR already exists in the revenue domain.

These very unlikely to happen in [CONFERENCE], most likely post DIAS fix if customer wants to deploy model:
 3. STRING types on numeric columns (both ECM and MVM): Things like length_of_stay, rooms_sold, bed_count, duration_minutes are typed as STRING across about 150 columns. The metric views handle it with CAST() but any customer writing their own SQL will hit type
errors. Won't surface in a demo but will be a real problem at deployment.

4. Missing FK from reservation_booking to room_type (both ECM and MVM): room_type_requested is stored as free text instead of linking to inventory.room_type. This is one of the most important joins in hospitality analytics. It's in next_vibes.txt but not in the release notes.

  One suggestion for a maybe future version: the model has sentiment scoring and lifetime value already, which is great. Adding a guest
  predictive score table (churn risk, upsell propensity, next-best-offer) and topic extraction on reviews/feedback would round out the
  AI/ML story nicely, especially for [CONFERENCE] conversations around AI in hospitality.
