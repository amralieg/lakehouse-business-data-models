Overview
The NGO Lakehouse Data Model is a Vibe Modelling Agent-generated artifact for nonprofit / humanitarian organizations, shipping in two tiers:
MVM: 12 domains, 31 subdomains, 141 tables, 6,093 attributes, 1,413 FKs, 114 metric views
ECM: 15 domains, 46 subdomains, 302 tables, 11,608 attributes, 1,821 FKs, 135 metric views
ECM adds three corporate-supporting domains (communication, safeguarding, technology), comprehensive M:N junction tables, and expanded reference data. Both tiers share attribute depth and naming conventions, supporting an MVM-to-ECM growth path without restructuring. Agent's deterministic quality score for ECM: 68/100, with 85 warnings and 37 actionable findings in next_vibes.txt. Ships as SQL DDL, RDF/Turtle ontology, DBML diagram, pre-built metric views, and CSV/Excel docs. Review scope: domain coverage, structural integrity, and Databricks platform fit - not line-by-line schema validation, metric SQL, or ontology verification. Lens is UN-agency / INGO because that profile exercises nearly every domain.
---
What the Model Does Well
Sector vocabulary is practitioner-grade. The donor and grant schemas use real humanitarian-finance artifacts: IATI organization identifiers (format-validated), OECD-DAC member flags, ODA eligibility, NICRA / indirect cost rates with cognizant-agency tracking, single-audit entities, GDPR consent dates and flags, LAI prospect scoring, wealth-screening provider references, and CRM source-system references (Salesforce Nonprofit Cloud, Raiser's Edge NXT). Recognizable to working donor-relations and grants-compliance professionals.
Two-tier MVM/ECM design. MVM minimizes M:N junctions in favor of direct FKs, excludes corporate back-office, and presents a deployable starting point. ECM preserves attribute depth across more entities with junction tables and corporate-supporting domains. Correct pattern for incremental Databricks customer onboarding.
Humanitarian operational footprint. The field domain (country_office, field_deployment, project_site, cluster_coordination, emergency, sitrep, security_incident, access_constraint) maps cleanly to OCHA-coordinated humanitarian operations as first-class concepts, not generic project attributes.
Safeguarding / PSEA coverage (ECM). Dedicated safeguarding domain includes safeguarding_incident, investigation, alleged_perpetrator, survivor_record, survivor_support_plan, focal_point, psea_policy, psea_network, community_awareness_session, and donor_safeguarding_requirement. More comprehensive than any open NGO data model surveyed; reflects post-2018 sector reforms under the UN SG's PSEA bulletin. Material differentiator for UN, [EU humanitarian funder], and major bilateral donor compliance.
MEL design reflects RBM. Logframe, indicator, indicator target/result, MEAL plan, theory of change, evaluation, evaluation finding, qualitative record, data quality assessment, and DHIS2 aggregate reporting. Correct shape for Results-Based Management used by [major UN agency], USAID and FCDO grantees, and major multilaterals.
Beneficiary identity is protection-aware. registrant as SSOT, layered with household, household_member, vulnerability_profile, protection_flag, consent_record, referral, case_record / case_action. ECM adds biometric_record, displacement_history, community_intervention - relevant for [UN refugee agency]-aligned and displaced-population workflows.
Column-level documentation. Most attributes carry detailed business-justification comments citing applicable standards (IATI, ASC 958, Form 990). Makes schemas largely self-documenting, which materially benefits downstream AI agents - the model's stated purpose.
---
Required Missing Details
Structural fixes flagged by the agent's own analysis. next_vibes.txt surfaces 37 actionable findings; the highest-impact:
program.program is completely isolated (zero inbound, zero outbound FKs) - blocks every program-rollup query (Priority 2 in next_vibes.txt).
22 unlinked FK columns (e.g. program.program.donor_id, program.program.implementing_partner_id, program.program.humanitarian_response_plan_id) named like FKs but lacking foreign_key_to references.
13 cross-domain duplicate-name SSOT violations: field.field_team vs volunteer.volunteer_team; mel.mel_logframe vs program.program_logframe; donor.donor_fund vs finance.finance_fund; grant.grant_amendment vs program.program_amendment; compliance.compliance_incident vs safeguarding.safeguarding_incident.
102 self-referencing FK warnings; one duplicate-stub table (volunteer.volunteer_deployment2); 731 STRING *_code / *_number / *_key attributes without FK to lookup tables.
Most are mechanical and resolvable in a single vibe iteration.
PII classification tags. Agent flags 444 attributes matching person-data patterns without `pii_` classification tags. Humanitarian beneficiary data carries higher-than-commercial sensitivity (protection status, displacement, vulnerability, biometrics, case notes). Required: classification tags (pii_beneficiary_protected, pii_donor, pii_staff, pii_de_identified) mapped to Unity Catalog tag values, plus a documented masking-policy set in the README. Customers cannot apply dynamic masking, RLS, or pass a donor audit without this.
HACT framework is not first-class. [major UN agency], [UN development agency], [UN population agency], and [UN food agency] operate under the Harmonized Approach to Cash Transfers. The model implies HACT via capacity_assessment and partner_compliance but the actual artifacts are missing: macro_assessment, micro_assessment (with the risk rating that determines transfer modality), programme_visit, spot_check, scheduled_audit, face_form (quarterly liquidation), and a first-class IP transfer modality enum (Direct Cash Transfer / Reimbursement / Direct Payment). Single largest content gap for UN-agency deployments.
Cash and Voucher Assistance (CVA) to beneficiaries. CVA is ~20%+ of global humanitarian assistance and growing. Distinct from cash-to-IPs (covered by HACT above). Missing: transfer modality enum (cash / voucher / in-kind / hybrid), Minimum Expenditure Basket reference table, Financial Service Provider entity (M-Pesa, RedRose, ATM card networks), end-user verification.
Cold chain and vaccine-specific data. For health-focused humanitarian deployments: missing cold_chain_equipment, batch_lot with expiry_date and who_pq_status, temperature_excursion events, vaccine_vial_monitor_state (VVM stages 1-4), and Gavi co-financing tracking.
UN programme hierarchy. Missing country_programme_document (CPD), unsdcf_outcome, strategic_plan_goal_area ([major UN agency]: Survive and Thrive, Learn, Protection, Safe and Clean Environment, Equitable Chance in Life), explicit SDG indicator alignment, and humanitarian_response_plan (anticipated by orphan FK program.program.humanitarian_response_plan_id).
Accounting framework framing. Finance domain comments reference ASC 958 (US GAAP for nonprofits) and Form 990 (US IRS). UN agencies report under IPSAS; larger INGOs operate under multiple frameworks. Required: configurable framework references in column comments, or explicit dual framing with IPSAS recognized alongside US GAAP.
Databricks deployment patterns. The model is a deployable SQL schema but not a deployable Databricks lakehouse. Add to the README:

MVM/ECM placement and stub reconciliation. Promote to MVM for humanitarian deployments: finance.exchange_rate, finance.bank_reconciliation, supply.inkind_donation, partnership.partner_risk_register, partnership.partner_compliance, field.pdm_survey, the entire safeguarding domain, donor.soft_credit. Reconcile MVM-only stub tables with their ECM equivalents: beneficiary.needs_assessment vs beneficiary.beneficiary_needs_assessment; field.distribution_line vs field.field_distribution_line; partnership.agreement vs partnership.partnership_agreement. ECM must be a true superset.
Operational systems-of-record documentation. Comments anchor on Salesforce Nonprofit Cloud and Raiser's Edge NXT - correct for National Committee CRMs but not UN-agency back-offices. UN agencies and large INGOs run SAP / SAP S/4HANA ([major UN agency]: VISION), eTools (programme management), InSight (BI), RAM, eZHACT, ICON (procurement), DHIS2, Kobo Toolbox, and Primero (child protection). Expand operational systems-of-record commentary in table descriptions to reflect the UN-agency stack.
