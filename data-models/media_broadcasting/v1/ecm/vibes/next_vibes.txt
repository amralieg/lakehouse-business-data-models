### Cross-cutting design choices

- **Lakehouse-ready DDL:** Unity-Catalog-style three-level namespace (`media_broadcasting_<scope>`.`<domain>`.`<table>`) with table comments, column comments, and inline regex validation.
- **Standards-aligned identifiers:** EIDR (`^10.5240/...`), ISAN, ISRC, ISWC, EBUCore, Dublin Core, TVAnytime are first-class.
- **Real vendor anchors:** Tables reference Dalet Galaxy (MAM), Rightsline (rights), Ericsson MediaFirst (playout), Wide Orbit (traffic), Akamai (CDN), Nielsen (measurement), Adobe Experience Platform (audience), SAP S/4HANA CO (finance), Salesforce Media Cloud (sales). Excellent for realistic demos.
- **Workload modes covered:** Linear (ATSC/DVB/QAM), OTT (HLS/MPEG-DASH/ABR/DRM), FAST channels, MVPD/vMVPD carriage, retransmission consent, dynamic ad insertion (SCTE-35), VOD windowing.
- **Regulatory coverage:** FCC, Ofcom, GDPR, CCPA, COPPA, SOX, PCI DSS, EBU R128, ATSC A/85 all referenced in attribute semantics.
- **Bridge tables:** MVM minimises M:N tables (direct FKs); ECM materialises ~30+ association tables (e.g., `collection_membership`, `platform_device_certification`, `crew_call` DOOD).
- **Generated companion artefacts per variant:** combined per-domain SQL DDL, metric-view SQL, sample CSVs, RDF/Turtle ontology, DBML diagram, Excel workbook, release notes.

## 3. What's Strong

1. **Content SSOT done right.** `title` → `series` → `season` → `episode` → `version` with EIDR/ISAN/ISRC + EBUCore/TVAnytime/Dublin Core metadata profiles is a textbook content master, well-suited to multi-platform demos.
2. **Rights model is best-in-class for the proposed scope.** Separation of `license_agreement` (contract) → `grant` (entitlement) → `availability_window` → `holdback` → `royalty_rule`/`royalty_statement` → `residual` mirrors how Rightsline/FilmTrack actually work.
3. **Distribution covers the full topology.** Linear (ATSC/DVB/QAM), OTT (HLS/DASH/ABR/DRM), FAST channels, MVPD/vMVPD with retransmission consent, SCTE-35/DAI, CDN per-PoP, device certification — this is rare to see in one model.
4. **D2C streaming is end-to-end.** `subscriber` + `subscription` + `entitlement` + `playback_session` + `churn_event` + `offer/win_back_offer` gives you the full SVOD lifecycle for churn/recommender demos out of the box.
5. **Talent and residuals are first-class.** `talent_contract`, `compensation_structure`, `guild_affiliation` (SAG-AFTRA/WGA/DGA), `cba_rate_card`, `residual_payment` — the streaming-era residuals story is increasingly important and the bones are here.
6. **Compliance is genuinely useful.** EAS log, closed-caption record, political ad disclosure, FCC public file, accessibility obligation — these *are* the FCC-mandated artefacts.
7. **Vendor-anchored attribute comments.** Many comments explicitly cite Dalet/Rightsline/Wide Orbit/Ericsson MediaFirst/Akamai/Nielsen — perfect for demo storytelling.
8. **DDL hygiene.** Inline column regex (`^10.5240/...`, `^[A-Z]{2}[A-Z0-9]{3}[0-9]{7}$`), enum candidates flagged for reference promotion, comments explain *business* purpose not just data type.

## 4. Coverage Gaps & Recommendations

Ordered by impact on demo realism for the highest-value M&B sub-verticals.

### A. High-impact additions

#### A1. News operations (MISSING domain)
News organisations are explicitly in the business description but there is no news-specific subdomain. Linear/OTT news plus digital publishing are huge segments ([major US linear/digital news org], [major public-service broadcaster], [major news agency], etc.) and need:

| Suggested table | Domain | Why |
|---|---|---|
| `news_story` | `content` (new `news_room` subdomain) or new `newsroom` domain | Story slug/dateline/byline/wire-source/embargo/correction lifecycle |
| `wire_ingest` | `newsroom` | Inbound feed from [major news agency]/AP/AFP/Bloomberg; raw → editorial workflow |
| `byline_credit` | `newsroom` | Reporter/photographer/editor credit (distinct from `talent_credit`) |
| `correction_record` | `compliance` | Editor's note, retraction, FCC/Ofcom-mandated on-air correction |
| `breaking_news_event` | `newsroom` | Crash-out/special-report trigger feeding `program_rundown` |
| `editorial_workflow_state` | `newsroom` | Draft → assigned → filed → edited → cleared → published |

#### A2. Sports rights & event production (MISSING)
"Sports" is enumerated as a series_type but has none of the entities sports broadcasters actually run on:

| Suggested table | Domain | Why |
|---|---|---|
| `sports_league` / `sports_competition` | `content` (new `live_events` subdomain) | NFL/NBA/UEFA — root of sports rights hierarchy |
| `sports_event` (game/match) | `content` | Per-event metadata: home/away, venue, season, date, status |
| `sports_team` / `athlete` | `talent` or `content` | Roster, jersey number, performance-rights flag |
| `event_blackout_rule` | `rights` | DMA/territory blackout (extends existing `rights_blackout_rule`) |
| `betting_data_feed` | `distribution` or new | Increasingly material — odds APIs, live data partners (Genius/Sportradar) |
| `multi_angle_feed` | `distribution` | ISO/4K/replay/skycam — distinct from main feed |

#### A3. Music & label catalog (PARTIAL)
The business description names "music labels" but `music_sync_license` is the only music-specific table. For a label-style demo you need recording-vs-composition separation:

| Suggested addition | Where | Why |
|---|---|---|
| `composition` (with ISWC) vs `master_recording` (with ISRC) | `content` `asset_catalog` | Mirrors industry split — different rights owners |
| `release` (album/EP/single) with UPC/EAN | `content` | DSP-facing catalog unit |
| `publisher` + `pro_affiliation` (ASCAP/BMI/SESAC/PRS) | `partner` or `rights` | Performance royalty routing |
| `neighbouring_rights_claim` | `rights` | SoundExchange-style non-interactive digital |
| `mechanical_royalty_report` (MLC) | `rights` | US Music Modernization Act flows |
| `dsp_usage_report` (Spotify/Apple/YouTube Music) | `audience` or `rights` | ISRC-keyed streaming counts |

#### A4. Programmatic / addressable advertising (PARTIAL)
Linear/DAI is rich but programmatic primitives are missing. The 2026 ad-tech story is increasingly CTV programmatic:

| Suggested addition | Where | Why |
|---|---|---|
| `dsp` / `ssp` / `exchange` master | `sales.advertising_operations` | Buy/sell side identity |
| `private_marketplace_deal` (PMP deal ID) | `sales` | Programmatic-direct & PMP |
| `bid_request` / `bid_response` (sampled) | `sales` or `audience` | OpenRTB telemetry for win-rate / clearing-price demos |
| `clean_room_match` | `audience` | Data clean room (LiveRamp/InfoSum/Habu) joins for measurement |
| `frequency_cap_state` | `distribution.platform_operations` | Already alluded to in `dai_configuration`, surface as table |
| `viewability_record` (MRC/IAB) | `audience` | Viewability/IVT — required for upfront guarantee reconciliation |

#### A5. Participation & talent backend (MISSING)
Residuals are well-handled, but high-end talent deals also carry **gross/net participation, deferred comp, bonus triggers, contingent comp** — distinct from residuals:

| Suggested addition | Where |
|---|---|
| `participation_definition` (gross/adjusted-gross/net) | `talent.agreement_management` |
| `participation_statement` | `talent` or `rights` |
| `deferred_compensation` | `talent` |
| `bonus_trigger` (box-office, viewership, award) | `talent` |

#### A6. Recommendation/personalisation feature store (PARTIAL)
`audience_profile` is there but the *features* feeding a recommender aren't surfaced. Adding these makes the model demo-ready for Databricks recommendation accelerators:

| Suggested addition | Where |
|---|---|
| `content_feature_vector` (embedding/topic/mood) | `audience` or new `personalization` subdomain |
| `viewer_feature_vector` | `audience` |
| `recommendation_event` (slate served + click-through) | `distribution` `platform_operations` |
| `ab_test_assignment` | `distribution` |

### B. Medium-impact additions

| Topic | Suggestion |
|---|---|
| **Social/UGC distribution** | Add `social_distribution_outlet` (YouTube/TikTok/IG) and `clip_distribution_event` — broadcasters increasingly distribute via these channels |
| **Podcast** | Podcast appears only as a rights enum; a `podcast_show` / `podcast_episode` / `podcast_dynamic_ad_insertion` mini-subdomain is justified — IAB-DAAST/Megaphone-style |
| **Translation/dubbing vendor workflow** | `localization` is good; add `dubbing_session` / `voice_actor_assignment` for end-to-end localisation cost/quality demos |
| **AI-generated content lineage** | A `content_provenance` table capturing C2PA / generative-AI flags is a 2026 must-have for compliance (EU AI Act, FCC AI ad disclosure) |
| **Cybersecurity / piracy** | `anti_piracy_takedown` and `watermark_forensic_match` — large studios run this as a domain |
| **Affiliate station group** | If the demo targets a station group (Sinclair/Nexstar/Tegna), `affiliate_station`, `network_clearance`, `local_avail_inventory` are missing |
| **Customer support / contact centre** | For D2C demos: `support_case`, `csat_survey` (especially for churn analytics) |

### C. Structural / hygiene suggestions

1. **Promote stripped ENUM-REF-CANDIDATEs to reference tables consistently.** Many columns carry `[ENUM-REF-CANDIDATE: ... — N candidates stripped; promote to reference product]`. MVM intentionally minimises lookups; ECM should fully materialise them so analytics joins are deterministic.
2. **Reconcile MVM-only "stub" tables flagged in the top-level readme.** E.g. `dispute` exists only in MVM under `billing.document_management` while ECM has `billing_dispute` under `invoice_management` — pick one to avoid drift.
3. **`audience.advertiser_commitments` placement is confusing.** Upfront/scatter commitments and demographic-target/guarantee belong in `sales`. Audience should be measurement only; commitments are a sales artefact. Consider moving.
4. **Cross-domain timestamp/lineage standardisation.** A column-naming standard for `_created_at`/`_updated_at`/`_source_system`/`_source_id` would let medallion (bronze→silver→gold) demos lineage-trace consistently.
5. **`technology` domain only in ECM is too aggressive a cut for MVM.** A linear broadcaster cannot demo without at least `broadcast_facility` and `transmission_equipment`. Consider promoting a minimal 2-table version into MVM.
6. **`workforce` domain ECM-only is correct** — the `talent` domain covers above-the-line; workforce HR is a corporate concern.
7. **Add explicit fact/dim labelling** in the model.json `data_type` field (it already has `master_data`/`reference_data`/`transactional_data`/`association_data`) — call out which transactional tables are intended as fact tables for star-schema metric views.
8. **RDF/OWL alignment with EBU CCDM URIs** is shipped under `ontology/` — verify the class IRIs use `http://www.ebu.ch/metadata/ontologies/ebucore` namespace where applicable, so consumers can federate with existing EBU-based catalogs.

## 6. Recommended Next Steps

Order of priority for an iteration on the data model that maximally improves demo coverage:

1. **Add a `newsroom` domain** (5–7 tables) — A1 above. This unlocks news broadcaster demos which the current model can't realistically run.
2. **Add a `live_events` / `sports` subdomain under `content`** (4–6 tables) — A2.
3. **Split music: `composition` vs `master_recording` + publishing entities** — A3, even 4–5 new tables changes the music-label demo profile dramatically.
4. **Add programmatic primitives in `sales`** (PMP deal, DSP/SSP master, sampled bid telemetry, viewability) — A4. Targets the CTV-programmatic conversation that is everywhere in 2026.
5. **Promote a minimal `technology` slice into MVM** — at least `broadcast_facility` and `transmission_equipment`, otherwise a linear-broadcaster demo cannot stand up.
6. **Move `advertiser_commitments` from `audience` to `sales`** — small move, large clarity gain.
7. **Reconcile MVM/ECM "stub" duplications** (e.g., `dispute` vs. `billing_dispute`) flagged in the readme — drift risk.
8. **Surface DAI granularity into MVM** — `dai_event` is ECM-only but FAST/AVOD monetisation demos hinge on it.

## 7. Questions for the Model Owner

These would let me sharpen the recommendations:

1. **Target persona for demos:** Is the primary persona a (a) large diversified studio (Disney/WBD/Paramount), (b) pure-play SVOD (Netflix-like), (c) traditional broadcaster (NBC/[major public-service broadcaster]), (d) station group (Sinclair/Nexstar), (e) news org ([major news agency]/AP), or (f) music label? The current model leans broadcaster + SVOD; if a music label or newsroom demo is in scope, A1/A3 are mandatory not optional.
2. **Programmatic vs. linear emphasis:** Should we deepen the programmatic/CTV side (A4) or keep the model focused on the linear/DAI hybrid story?
3. **MVM minimalism principle:** Is the goal to keep MVM at ~186 tables, or are targeted additions welcome where they unlock a major demo theme?
4. **Ontology consumers:** Are external consumers expected to federate the shipped Turtle ontology with EBU CCDM IRIs? If so, IRI alignment is worth a verification pass.
5. **Sample data scope:** The repo references `samples/` with one CSV per data product — for which variant (MVM, ECM, or both) are samples being generated?
6. **Region/language emphasis:** Anything specific to EMEA broadcasters (Ofcom, EBU R128, DVB-T2) vs. US (FCC, ATSC) that should be foregrounded as separate demo variants?

## 8. Bottom Line

The proposed data model is **substantially above average for an industry data model that targets demos and apps** — it covers the linear ↔ OTT ↔ FAST ↔ D2C topology in a single coherent schema, anchors on real industry identifiers (EIDR/ISAN/ISRC/ISWC), references real vendor systems by name (Dalet, Rightsline, Wide Orbit, Ericsson MediaFirst, Akamai, Nielsen, Adobe AEP, SAP, Salesforce), and ships a Lakehouse-ready DDL with Unity-Catalog namespacing, rich comments, and inline regex validation. The MVM/ECM split is a sensible packaging choice.

The most material gaps are **newsroom**, **sports/live-events**, **music-label catalog**, **programmatic/clean-room ad-tech**, and **content provenance (AI/C2PA)** — each unlocks a distinct demo theme. Implementing items A1–A4 would convert this from "broadcaster + SVOD demo model" into a **defensible reference model across all six M&B personas** (studio, SVOD, broadcaster, station group, newsroom, music label).
